Coastal Home Design by Flagg

I'm Block A Flagship Properties is a privately held real estate development and brokerage company located in Coronado California. Founded in 1975, the company has developed, marketed and sold quality residential and office building developments throughout Southern California. Since 1999, Flagship has specialized in "Coastal Homes" primarily in Coronado, and has completed 30 homes on the Island. FLAGG COASTAL HOMES, the separate building company, was founded in 2007 to complement Flagship's development and brokerage activities. FLAGG is a licensed contractor specializing in Design/Build activities.

FLAGG has been an innovator on the island selecting new concrete composite siding PVC and poly-styrene trim. These new materials were selected to withstand the direct sun and coastal environment.

Since so much of Coronado living takes place outdoors in the mild Mediterranean climate. FLAGG makes a concerted effort to extend the interiors into the porch, the deck, the back yard, the lawn, and the patio.

The professionals at Flagg, particularly Carrie and John OBrien, Mark Blore of BSC, Martin Brown of St. Lucia Builders and Beth Delano of Shipshape Interiors wish you many happy memories and special occasions in your FLAGG COASTAL HOME in Coronado.

FLAGG COASTAL HOMES is a licensed California General Contractor, Lic. 898 050, registered with the Department of Corporations as Flagg Inc. The company is organized as a Design/build Company specializing in developing high quality Coastal Homes primarily in the island community of Coronado, CA . Flagg has designed and built 30 homes in Coronado since 2000.

Whether overlooking the bay, or located on a quiet tree-lined street mid island, FLAGG homes feature light paint colors.

Coastal Construction

I'm Block A Pre-construction and Design Services Contract: Flagship and its related homebuilding company, FLAGG Coastal Homes are experienced builders, subscribing to the notion that the most important step in constructing a new home is developing a quality plan. Not only must the site conditions be taken into account, but neighborhood, street presence, engineering and environmental conditions and importantly budget and schedule are all essential aspects of the plan. We are often asked to address and embrace the coastal charm of Coronado relying on our experience in building in this very unique environment.

Plan Approval & Permitting: Based on our experience in developing and re-developing more than 30 homes in Coronado, we have a keen understanding of the permitting process for Coronado. According to the complexity or size of the site, Flagship will also develop a schematic landscape and hardscape plan.

Schedule and Budget: Also included in the Pre-Construction Phase, Flagg will develop a "Preliminary Budget and Building Schedule." This budget will contain a number of allowances for items such as cabinets, countertops, appliances and the like. Both Flagship, the development company; and Flagg Coastal Homes, the licensed contractor will utilize experiences from the past 30 "Coastal Homes" in Coronado to formulate a better, more refined budget and schedule. That experience translates into a comfort level and assurances that the home is in the hands of experienced professionals.

Construction Activities: Upon receipt of a building permit Flagship's role in pre-development will terminate and FLAGG Inc. the licensed and insured general contractor will execute a Construction Contract. Based on our experience, the "Cost Plus a Fee" contract including an appropriate contingency, allows the most efficient manner of work progression and minimizes the use of "Change Orders" Recent experience in Coronado suggests that FLAGG is able to complete a new Coastal Home from start work to final permit in 8-11 months

Coronado

This page is all about Coronado. Will include photos.

I'm Block A Coronado, also known as Coronado Island, is an affluent resort city located in San Diego County, California, across San Diego Bay from downtown San Diego.[4] Its population was 24,697 at the 2010 census,[2][3] up from 24,100 at the 2000 census. Coronado lies on a peninsula connected to the mainland by a 10-mile (16 km) isthmus called the Silver Strand (locally, The Strand.) Locals sometimes call Coronado Nado or Coronado Island, and they denote the core living and business area as The Village. In May 2012, Dr. Stephen Leatherman, Director of the Laboratory for Coastal Research, ranked Coronado Beach as the best beach in the United States.[5]

Coronado is Spanish for "the crowned one", and thus it is nicknamed The Crown City. There have been three ships of the United States Navy named after the city, including the USS Coronado (LCS-4).
